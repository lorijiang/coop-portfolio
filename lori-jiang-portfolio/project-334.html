Project Brief

In this academic project, teams were tasked with designing a new app to address a target audience: seniors 75 years or older.

Problem

It is difficult for older Chinese immigrants to find community in Canada.

As important as community is in East Asian culture, many older Chinese immigrant adults struggle to form meaningful social interactions due to language barriers and cultural differences.

Solution

Create an app for older Chinese immigrant adults to find community events to participate in.

Creating an mobile app that allows older Chinese immigrants to find activities in their community that help them socialize and also maintain their connection to their culture while away from home.

Starting Broad

We started off researching potential problem spaces for adults aged 75 years and older more generally. Then, we looked into our personal lives for inspiration found that our team members all had experience living with our Chinese grandparents who were immigrants, which drove us to want to design an app to benefit them in some way.

Anecdotal Insights

Older adults are at higher risk to experience loneliness, which puts them at risk for both mental and physical health issues.

Ideate

Wireframe Sketches

I created quick wireframe sketches to explore what essential features and screens we would need to incorporate into the app. Using our user personas to guide our design thinking, we thought that there would need to be:

  • language translation feature to overcome language barriers near beginning of user flow
  • simple events page as the home page
  • personal profile to allow for personalized event suggestions
  • ability to save events of interest
  • detailed information about the event's activity, location, and date

    Key Design Decisions

    Language Options

    The first screen allows the user to select their preferred language upfront before further interacting with the app.

    Key Design Decisions

    Tap, Not Scroll

    A tap interaction is used to navigate through the pages rather than a scroll which could be difficult for older adults with hand dexterity issues.

    Key Design Decisions

    Cultural Events

    The events are curated towards cultural activities that older Chinese immigrants might miss from back home, including popular community-based activities like mahjong, taichi, or food markets.

    Key Design Decisions

    In-App Map

    It can be cumbersome to move in and out of the app to find directions, so we proposed a built-in map function to help decide whether the destination is accessible through walking or transit, as many do not drive.

    Key Design Decisions

    UI Personalized with Chinese Motifs

  • Especially with older generations, many Chinese people believe that using red and certain patterns symbolizes luck.
  • Detailed information pages were designed to look like old Chinese flip calenders that older adults will likely have used in the past.
  • Event widgets on home page designed to look like mahjong tiles

    Reflection

    What I learned

    While there is a lot of room for improvement in the app's visual and UI design, I was able to learn a lot about accessible design and how to work within stricter design constraints.

    Things that can be improved

    Due to time and resources constraints, our user research was limited to a small sample size and relied a lot on secondary research. If I were to continue this project, I would like to gain a deeper understanding of our target audience through more user-interviews.
